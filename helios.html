Your risk isn't one thing — so one tool was never enough

An attack is a chain: a leaked password, used against an exposed login, to get a foothold, then a quiet walk across an unpatched internal network — and misconfigured cloud along the way.

Point tools show you one link at a time. Helios connects them. External ASM, Internal ASM, CTI, and CSPM all run against one shared asset inventory, in one dashboard — so you see the whole chain and can break it before it reaches anything that matters.

In plain terms: Helios is a cloud (SaaS) platform you log in to from any web browser. It watches four things at once — what you expose to the internet, what's reachable inside your network, what attackers already know about you, and how your cloud is configured — and tells you, continuously, what to fix first. You can run it yourself, or have our team operate it for you.

One attack. Every angle.

A real intrusion is rarely a single event. It's a chain — and Helios watches every link.

1 CTI They learn

A password shows up in a breach dump. Threat intel flags it against your domain before anyone uses it.

2 External ASM They get in

That credential is tried against a login portal you forgot was exposed. External ASM already found — and flagged — that portal.

See only one link, and you're guessing about the rest. See all four — in one place — and you break the chain before it reaches anything that matters.

Frequently Asked Questions

Plain-English answers to the questions we hear most often.

What is Helios by Isaphia, in plain English?

Helios is a single security platform that shows you everything an attacker could use against you: what you expose to the internet (External ASM), what's reachable once someone gets inside your network (Internal ASM), what's already being said, sold, or leaked about you online (CTI), and how your cloud accounts are configured (CSPM). It all lives in one dashboard, and you log in from a web browser.

What modules make up Helios?

Four: External ASM (your internet-facing footprint), Internal ASM (assets and legacy systems inside your network, discovered by lightweight collectors), CTI (asset-aware cyber threat intelligence), and CSPM (cloud security posture management across AWS, Azure, and GCP).

Do I have to buy all four modules?

No. Most customers start with External ASM and add Internal ASM, CTI, and CSPM as they mature. The modules share one asset inventory and one dashboard, so each one you add makes the others more useful.

Is Helios a SaaS product?

Yes. Helios is delivered as Software-as-a-Service — you log in from any web browser. You can run it yourself (Self-Service SaaS) or have the Isaphia team operate it for you (Managed Service).

Do I need to install anything?

External ASM, CTI, and CSPM are agentless — nothing runs on your machines. Internal ASM is the exception: it uses lightweight, safe collectors deployed inside your network to map what's behind the firewall. CSPM connects to your cloud accounts with read-only access.

How is Helios different from buying separate tools?

Attacks are a chain — a leaked password used against an exposed login to get a foothold, then lateral movement across an unpatched internal network. Separate tools show you one link at a time. Helios correlates all four views against one shared asset inventory, so you see the whole chain and can break it before it reaches anything that matters.

How does pricing work?

Pricing scales with the size of your attack surface and the modules you enable, and whether you choose Self-Service SaaS or the Managed Service. Users are unlimited within your tenant — no per-seat fees. Contact us for a scoped quote.
